# AI Maturity Assessment Framework - Methodology

## Framework Overview

This framework evaluates organizational AI capability maturity by analyzing job posting patterns. It provides an objective, data-driven assessment based on the types of roles organizations hire, their seniority distribution, and functional integration patterns.

**Core Premise**: Hiring patterns reveal organizational AI strategy and maturity.

## Maturity Model (5 Levels)

### Level 1: Ad-hoc / Experimentation

**Characteristics**:
- Isolated AI roles without clear structure
- Pilot projects without cross-functional patterns
- No systematic approach to AI talent

**Hiring Indicators**:
- Sporadic AI-related hires
- No leadership positions
- Single-function concentration (typically IT/Engineering)
- Generic titles (e.g., "Data Analyst")

**Organizational State**: Exploring AI without strategic commitment

---

### Level 2: Foundational / Emerging

**Characteristics**:
- First wave of technical hires (data scientists, ML engineers)
- Limited scope and integration
- Foundation-building phase

**Hiring Indicators**:
- 1-20 AI roles
- Mostly junior/mid-level technical positions
- Concentrated in IT/Engineering functions
- Minimal business integration
- Basic ML titles (e.g., "Data Scientist", "ML Engineer")

**Organizational State**: Building technical foundation

---

### Level 3: Scaling / Functional Integration

**Characteristics**:
- Repeatable technical role hiring patterns
- Early cross-functional AI deployment
- Platform roles begin to emerge
- Junior leadership positions appear

**Hiring Indicators**:
- 20-100 AI roles
- Multiple business functions hiring AI talent (3-4 functions)
- First "Lead" or "Manager" AI roles
- Evidence of platform/infrastructure focus
- Some business-facing AI roles (e.g., "Product Data Scientist")

**Organizational State**: Systematically scaling AI capabilities

---

### Level 4: Enterprise Integration

**Characteristics**:
- AI embedded across multiple business functions
- Formal AI leadership established
- Platform and infrastructure maturity
- Responsible AI signals (ethics, governance)

**Hiring Indicators**:
- 100-300 AI roles
- Director/VP-level AI leadership
- Dedicated ML platform/infrastructure teams
- AI roles in non-technical functions (Product, Operations, Strategy)
- Ethics/governance positions emerging
- Specialized technical roles (e.g., "NLP Engineer", "ML Architect")

**Organizational State**: AI as enterprise capability

---

### Level 5: AI-Native / Transformational

**Characteristics**:
- AI drives core strategy, growth, and operating model
- Mature platform and governance structures
- Ecosystem-level impact (partnerships, industry influence)
- Business model transformation

**Hiring Indicators**:
- 300+ AI roles
- C-suite AI leadership (Chief AI Officer)
- AI roles spanning entire value chain (7+ functions)
- External-facing AI roles (partnerships, ecosystem)
- Industry thought leadership positions
- AI embedded in product DNA
- Distinguished researchers and AI Fellows

**Organizational State**: AI-native organization

---

## 8-Dimension Evaluation Framework

Each dimension scored on 1-5 scale based on evidence from job postings.

### Dimension 1: Technical Depth

**Definition**: Sophistication and specialization of technical AI roles

**Scoring**:
- **1**: Generic roles (e.g., "Data Analyst")
- **2**: Basic ML roles (e.g., "Data Scientist", "ML Engineer")
- **3**: Specialized roles (e.g., "NLP Engineer", "Computer Vision Engineer")
- **4**: Advanced specialization (e.g., "Research Scientist", "ML Architect")
- **5**: Cutting-edge research (e.g., "AI Research Director", "Distinguished Scientist")

**Evidence**: Job title specificity, advanced technologies mentioned, research positions

---

### Dimension 2: Technical Width

**Definition**: Spread of AI talent across business functions

**Scoring**:
- **1**: AI in 1 function only
- **2**: AI in 2 functions
- **3**: AI in 3-4 functions
- **4**: AI in 5-6 functions
- **5**: AI in 7+ functions (enterprise-wide)

**Evidence**: Function diversity, cross-functional roles

---

### Dimension 3: Business Integration

**Definition**: AI embedded in product, operations, and strategy roles

**Scoring**:
- **1**: Pure technical roles only
- **2**: First business-adjacent roles (Analytics, BI)
- **3**: Product-integrated AI (e.g., "Product Data Scientist", "ML Product Manager")
- **4**: Operations-integrated AI (e.g., "AI Operations Manager")
- **5**: Strategy-level AI (e.g., "AI Strategy Director", "Chief AI Officer")

**Evidence**: "Product", "Strategy", "Operations" combined with AI/ML in titles

---

### Dimension 4: Governance & Leadership

**Definition**: Presence of senior leaders and governance structures

**Scoring**:
- **1**: No leadership roles
- **2**: Team Leads only
- **3**: Managers/Senior Managers
- **4**: Directors/Senior Directors
- **5**: VP+ / C-suite

**Evidence**: Leadership keywords (Lead, Manager, Director, VP, Chief), governance roles

---

### Dimension 5: Platformisation

**Definition**: Platform, infrastructure, and MLOps capabilities

**Critical Rule**: Only count roles with explicit AI/ML keywords

**Scoring**:
- **1**: No platform/infrastructure roles
- **2**: First infrastructure hires (DevOps with ML exposure)
- **3**: Dedicated MLOps roles (e.g., "MLOps Engineer", "ML Platform Engineer")
- **4**: ML Platform teams (e.g., "ML Infrastructure Manager")
- **5**: Mature platform organization (e.g., "VP of ML Platform")

**Evidence**: MLOps, ML Platform, ML Infrastructure keywords

---

### Dimension 6: Seniority Mix

**Definition**: Balance of junior vs. senior talent

**Scoring**:
- **1**: 100% junior roles
- **2**: Mostly junior (70%+ junior/mid-level)
- **3**: Balanced mix (40-60% senior)
- **4**: Senior-heavy (60%+ senior/leadership)
- **5**: Leadership-dominated (40%+ leadership roles)

**Calculation**: % of senior + leadership roles vs. total

---

### Dimension 7: Ecosystem

**Definition**: Partnerships, external collaboration, open innovation roles

**Scoring**:
- **1**: No ecosystem-facing roles
- **2**: First external-facing roles (e.g., "Developer Relations - AI")
- **3**: Partnership roles (e.g., "AI Partnership Manager")
- **4**: Ecosystem roles (e.g., "AI Ecosystem Lead", "Open Source ML Lead")
- **5**: Industry-shaping roles (e.g., "AI Standards Lead")

**Evidence**: "Partnership", "Alliance", "Ecosystem", "Developer Relations" in AI roles

---

### Dimension 8: Regulatory & Ethics

**Definition**: Ethics, compliance, and responsible AI focus

**Scoring**:
- **1**: No ethics/compliance roles
- **2**: First awareness (generic compliance with AI exposure)
- **3**: Dedicated roles (e.g., "AI Ethics Researcher", "Responsible AI Analyst")
- **4**: Formal programs (e.g., "Responsible AI Manager", "AI Governance Lead")
- **5**: Strategic priority (e.g., "VP of Responsible AI", "Chief Ethics Officer")

**Evidence**: "Ethics", "Responsible AI", "Fairness", "Governance", "Compliance" keywords

---

## Overall Maturity Calculation

### Step 1: Calculate Dimension Scores (1-5)
Calculate each of the 8 dimension scores based on job posting evidence.

### Step 2: Base Maturity Score
Average the 8 dimension scores.

### Step 3: Apply Scale Adjustments

**Scale Impact**:
- 300+ total jobs: +1.0 level boost (enterprise scale)
- 150-299 jobs: +0.5 level boost (rapid scaling)
- <50 jobs: -0.5 level reduction (limited scale)

**Growth Impact**:
- 2x+ year-over-year growth: +0.5 level boost

### Step 4: Final Maturity Level
Round to nearest 0.5 increment (levels can be 2.0, 2.5, 3.0, etc.)

**Overall Maturity = min(5, max(1, base_maturity + scale_adjustments))**

## Comparative Analysis

When comparing two companies:

### Scale Differentiation Rule
Companies with 5x+ job count differences should have 1+ level gap

### Volume-Based Benchmarks
- 300+ jobs: Minimum Level 3.5
- 150+ jobs: Minimum Level 3.0
- 50+ jobs: Minimum Level 2.5
- <50 jobs: Foundational (Level 2.0)

### Evidence Consistency
- Overall maturity must align with dimension scores
- Cannot have Level 4 overall with all dimensions at 2
- Maturity claims must cite specific job title evidence

## Use Cases

### 1. Competitive Benchmarking
**Scenario**: Compare your AI maturity against competitors

**Approach**:
- Collect job postings from 3-5 competitor companies
- Calculate maturity scores for each
- Identify dimension gaps
- Inform hiring strategy to close gaps

### 2. Temporal Trend Analysis
**Scenario**: Track AI maturity evolution over time

**Approach**:
- Analyze job postings year-over-year (2022, 2023, 2024)
- Identify maturity progression or stagnation
- Spot inflection points (e.g., first VP hire, platform team formation)
- Forecast future trajectory

### 3. M&A Due Diligence
**Scenario**: Assess AI capability of acquisition target

**Approach**:
- Analyze target's hiring patterns
- Identify AI talent concentration and depth
- Evaluate integration readiness with your organization
- Estimate talent retention risk

### 4. Talent Market Intelligence
**Scenario**: Understand AI hiring trends in your industry

**Approach**:
- Aggregate job postings across industry
- Identify emerging role types
- Benchmark salary ranges
- Inform talent acquisition strategy

## Customization Guide

### Industry-Specific Adaptations

**Healthcare**:
- Add dimension: "Clinical Integration" (AI in clinical workflows)
- Emphasize regulatory dimension (FDA, HIPAA compliance)

**Finance**:
- Add dimension: "Risk & Compliance" (AI for fraud detection, regulatory reporting)
- Emphasize governance dimension (model explainability, audit trails)

**Retail**:
- Add dimension: "Customer-Facing AI" (personalization, recommendation systems)
- Emphasize business integration (AI in marketing, supply chain)

### Custom Dimension Weights

If certain dimensions matter more to your organization:

```yaml
dimension_weights:
  technical_depth: 0.25       # High priority
  technical_width: 0.15
  business_integration: 0.20  # High priority
  governance: 0.15
  platformisation: 0.10
  seniority_mix: 0.05
  ecosystem: 0.05
  regulatory: 0.05            # Low priority
```

Weighted maturity = Σ(dimension_score × weight)

## Limitations

### Hiring Lag
Job postings may lag actual organizational capability by 3-6 months. Active projects may not yet be reflected in hiring.

### Public Data Only
Cannot assess internal AI projects, proprietary tools, or capabilities not reflected in job postings.

### Volume Bias
Larger companies naturally score higher on scale-adjusted metrics. Small, highly specialized AI teams may be undervalued.

### Keyword Dependence
Framework relies on keyword detection in job titles. Non-standard titles may be missed.
